Projects

Flea Flicker (MLH Local Hack Day 2014)

FleaFlicker is an Windows/OSX application and custom peripheral that allows you to flick a physical ball and view the results of the flick in a virtual game. It was designed and built at Local Hack Day 2014 with Tim Lupo. Currently, FleaFlicker works with a solo input device, but we are in the process of adding multiplayer capabilities, scorekeeping, etc. You can watch our demo here!

Handi-Hacked (Hack Gen Y 2015)


Handi-Hacked is a hardware hack developed along with Raphael Kats, Tim Lupo, Amy Sorto, and Ari Bercowitz. Using the Oculus Rift and Unity 3D, we were able to create a virtual reality environment which one could move around in. We also created a wooden base which was able to support a wheelchair and kept it stationary while the wheels were still mobile. This kind of setup allowed us to attach mice near the wheels in order to use them as inputs. The final product is a surreal virtual reality experience aimed at people who cannot easily manipulate a keyboard. We plan to expand on this idea and release the SDK's written at the event with the aspiration of producing/distributing these wheelchair inputs across the world.
